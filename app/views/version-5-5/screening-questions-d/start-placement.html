Adopt a child placed in your care

You can apply to adopt a child who's in your care following a court placement order.

They must have permanently for at least 10 weeks before the court will look at your application.

You can continue to fill in the application form before that time, though.

How to apply

Work with your social worker to fill in this form. You'll need information only they might have.

You'll need the child's:

  • full birth certificate
  • placement order details
Information you give about the child will only be seen by the court and social workers.

You'll also be asked about their:

  • birth parents, such as name, address and occupation
  • previous court proceedings
  • previous court proceedings for siblings or half-siblings

You'll also need to post the child's full birth certificate to the family court reviewing your application.

About you – things you might need to upload

You'll need to provide each applicant's:

  • passport photo page
  • driving licence

If relevant, you'll also need to upload:

  • your marriage or civil partnership certificate, in English or Welsh
  • your spouse or civil partner's death certificate
  • a decree absolute of divorce or decree of nullity of your marriage
  • a dissolution order or nullity order of your civil partnership
  • a decree of judicial separation
  • if you're using a different name from the one on your marriage or civil partnership certificate, evidence to explain the difference – for example, your deed poll
  • a copy of your UK visa
  • any passport pages that have UK immigration entry stamps
