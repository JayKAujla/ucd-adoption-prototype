Apply to adopt a child placed in your care

You can apply to adopt a child who's in your care following a court placement order.

The child must have lived with you for at least 10 weeks before you apply.

You can start your application and save your progress but cannot submit until the 10 week period has passed.

Before you start

1. Details about the child.

You'll need some documents and information about the child:

  • the child's full birth certificate (this includes details of the child's birth parents)
  • details from the child's placement order
  • the birth parents' names, addresses and occupations
  • details of any previous court proceedings involving the child, or the child's siblings or half siblings

The social worker will be able to help you get these details.

Information you give about yourselves and the child you want to adopt will only be seen by the court and social workers.

2. Details about you

For each of the people applying to adopt, you'll need:

  • passport or driving licence
  • marriage or civil partnership certificate, if you're married or in a civil partnership (in English or Welsh)
  • documents about any legal change of name (for example, a deed poll document)
  • UK visa, if you have one

If either of you is divorced or legally separated, you'll need a copy of the legal paperwork. For example, a decree absolute or dissolution order.

If either of you has a spouse or civil partner who has died, you'll need a copy of their death certificate.

If your spouse is physically or mental incapable of making an application, you will need to produce evidence of this, such as medical documents.
