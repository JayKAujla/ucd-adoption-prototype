Understanding 'parental responsibility'


In family law, parental responsibility means all the rights, duties, powers, responsibilities and authority that a parent has for the child.

If a child’s father and mother were married to each other when the child was born, each has parental responsibility.

If the parents were not married to each other at that time, the father can gain parental responsibility:

  • by registering the child’s birth jointly with the mother
  • by marrying the child’s mother
  • through a ‘parental responsibility agreement’ between him and the child’s mother which is registered with the court
  • by getting a court order for parental responsibility
