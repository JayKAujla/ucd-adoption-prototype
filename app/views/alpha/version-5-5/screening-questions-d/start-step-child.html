Apply to adopt your step-child

You can apply to adopt your partner's child even if their other parent is still alive.

You can only apply if:

  • you've lived with the child as a family for at least 6 months
  • you've given your local council at least 3 months' notice that you want to adopt
You should talk to the other parent about your wish to adopt. If they do not consent, a court will decide if your application is in the child's bests interests.

After you apply, the court will ask your local council to create a report on:

  • your partner
  • the child
  • the other parent

If given, the adoption court order gives you parental responsibility for the child, along with your partner.

How to apply

You'll need to post the child's long-form birth certificate. You can get a copy if you do not have the original.

You'll also need to upload 2 types of photo ID for yourself. For example, your passport's photo page and driving licence.

If relevant, you'll also need to upload:

  • the death certificate of the other parent
  • a copy of your UK visa
  • any passport pages that have UK immigration entry stamps
